About Me

My name is CJ, I am currently in a rotation program at Google, the Cloud Technical Residency program, and I am working as a Technical Account Manager for Unity, and a Strategic Cloud Engineer on an upcoming project that is TBA.

Where do I come from?

I was born and raised in Southern New Hampshire, just by the Maine border. I went to school in the Dover school system and grew up not having a lot. There were many opportunities I didn't get that a lot of others did, and I struggled my way into Plymouth State University, where I graduated a semester early with a Bachelor's in Computer Science. During the time I was enrolled there, I took up an internship with a small Managed Service Provider called Nessit, based in Somersworth NH, where I gained a lot of experience working with Networking Infrastructure, Systems Adminstration, and working in a Helpdesk role. During this time, I also worked for PSU's Classroom Technology Services team, troubleshooting most of the school's A/V problems.

My Path to Google

My life changed forever when in September of 2021 I got a call from a Google recruiter, asking me to interview for the Cloud Technical Residency program. At first, I thought this was fake, but I set up a call with the recruiter anyway and she told me about the program and all of the things it could offer me. Naturally, I dropped everything and accepted. I started the interview process, and focused on studying for the interviews for weeks. Now, I am not one to study; Having ADHD makes this a very challenging task for me, but I knew that this opportunity would be worth it, even if I didn't get the job. At least I could say that I tried. Needless to say, based on my GitHub profile, I got the job! It still wasn't smooth sailing, however. I had to completely uproot my life in New Hampshire to move to Atlanta, Georgia; A thousand miles away from my home, a completely unknown land where I knew no one. Leaving my family was one of the hardest things I have ever had to do, but it was worth it for a chance to improve myself, and eventually give back to them because they deserve the world, and if I can give even the littlest bit back to them, I think it's worth it.

Now, Why Do You Care
